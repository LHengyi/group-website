Research Projects

Cross-Layer Design for Cyber-Physical Systems Security This work aims at building secure cyber-physical systems considering various attack surfaces and limited computation and communication resources. Our work combines control-theoretic methods at the functional layer and cybersecurity techniques at the embedded platform layer. We have also worked on security-aware mapping for automotive systems.

Model-Based Software Synthesis for Reliable Embedded Systems This work aims at generating efficient and reliable implementations for embedded systems. We build functional and architectural models, and develop algorithms to map various functional models (synchronous block diagrams and synchronous finite state machines) onto various hardware platforms (single-core, multi-core and distributed platforms) to maximize reliability. The reliability here includes both long-time reliability (e.g., lifetime), and soft error tolerance.
